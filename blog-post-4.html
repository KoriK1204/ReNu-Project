iPhone 15 Pro vs iPhone 16: Full Breakdown

ReNu Tech Team March 10, 2026 7 min read

Home Blog iPhone 15 Pro vs iPhone 16: Full Breakdown

Still debating between the iPhone 15 Pro and the iPhone 16? You're not alone. Both are excellent phones — but they serve slightly different buyers. We lay out every meaningful difference so you can shop smarter and spend less.

Design & Build

The iPhone 15 Pro features a premium titanium frame — lighter and stronger than aluminum. The iPhone 16 uses aerospace-grade aluminum with the new Action Button, which was previously exclusive to the Pro line. Both phones feel premium, but the 15 Pro's titanium build has a distinctly higher-end feel in hand.

Performance

The iPhone 15 Pro runs on the A17 Pro chip, while the iPhone 16 uses the A18. In daily use — scrolling, apps, video, gaming — both are absolutely smooth and responsive. The A18 in the iPhone 16 has a slight edge in AI-heavy tasks and future-proofing, but the A17 Pro in the 15 Pro is still a powerhouse that won't slow down anytime soon.

Camera System

This is where the differences get interesting. The iPhone 15 Pro has a 48MP main sensor with a dedicated 12MP 3x telephoto lens — exceptional for portraits and zoom shots. The iPhone 16 brings improvements to computational photography and a new Camera Control button for quick access to shooting modes.

For most everyday photographers, both phones produce stunning results. If you shoot a lot of video professionally, the 15 Pro's ProRes recording capabilities still make it stand out.

Display

The iPhone 15 Pro features a ProMotion 120Hz display with always-on capability. The standard iPhone 16 has a 60Hz display — a noticeable difference when scrolling and animating. If screen smoothness matters to you, this is the most important spec to consider.

Price Comparison (Refurbished)

At ReNu Tech, a refurbished iPhone 15 Pro starts at $649, while a refurbished iPhone 16 starts at $699. The 15 Pro gives you a better display, a titanium build, and ProRes video for slightly less. For most buyers, it's the smarter value purchase.

Our Pick

If you want the premium experience, go with the refurbished iPhone 15 Pro. If you prefer the latest chip and the new Camera Control button, the iPhone 16 is worth the small premium. Either way, both are outstanding phones at a fraction of the new price.
